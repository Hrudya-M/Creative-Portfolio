Project One — Part 1

The Teardown

Content Strategy Audience Psychology Platform Pattern-Reading

Rentomojo's Instagram reels kept showing up in my feed, and they kept making me feel something before I registered the brand name. I picked five of their best-performing reels and broke each into its hook, content idea, storytelling structure, and brand integration. Two are unpacked in full below.

01

"POV: Living in a City"

"City. New job. Empty apartment."
Storytelling

Person moves into an empty apartment → realizes furniture costs are huge → feels overwhelmed → discovers Rentomojo → apartment transforms quickly. A clean problem → struggle → solution arc.

Brand Integration

Rentomojo appears only after the pain point is fully established — so it reads as a solution someone found, not a product being pushed.

What Caught Attention

The hook never mentions furniture. It places you inside a specific, common situation — curiosity does the rest.

Why it worked: high relatability, a strong emotional trigger, and a product that earns its place instead of interrupting the story.
02

"Why Buy When You Can Rent?"

"Spent ₹1.5 lakh furnishing your rented apartment?"
Storytelling

Show the high upfront expense → show the stress of ownership → introduce renting as the smarter alternative → end on savings and flexibility.

Brand Integration

The buy-vs-rent comparison leads naturally into Rentomojo's value proposition — no forced product placement required.

What Caught Attention

A number creates instant financial shock — an angle anyone can do the math on immediately.

Why it worked: challenges a default mindset, uses numbers people instantly understand, and creates an "aha" moment that's easy to share.
The Strategic Insight

Rentomojo almost never markets furniture. It markets freedom, flexibility, smart spending, and hassle-free urban life.

Across all five reels, the strongest content shares one structure: the audience sees their own problem first, and only afterward sees Rentomojo as the answer. That problem-first sequencing is what shaped the campaign concept below.

Problem-First Structure Lifestyle Over Features Brand As Solution, Not Hero

Project Two — The Brief

My Home Sayuk — UGC Script Set

Scriptwriting Dialogue & Pacing Short-Form Video Structure

The brief: a UGC/influencer script for My Home Sayuk, a residential community in Tellapur, Hyderabad — built to showcase its grandeur, lifestyle, and amenities, 90–120 seconds, in a voice that sounds like a real person talking, not an ad. Rather than write one script, I wrote four — each built around a different narrative device, to explore which structure would carry the brief's value-and-lifestyle goal best.

Four Approaches, One Brief

Each script uses the same real property facts — 12 towers, 25.37 acres, the resort-style clubhouse, sports facilities, kids' play area, and Tellapur's connectivity — but routes them through a different emotional engine.

SCRIPT 01

The Walkthrough

A straightforward discovery script — react to each space as you find it, build warmth gradually, close on sincerity.

Takeaway: argues value through cumulative detail. No single amenity is "the reason" — the accumulation makes the case.

SCRIPT 02

The Bet

She bets ₹50 she'll find one flaw in the property — and visibly loses the bet, scene by scene.

Takeaway: credibility through reluctant admission. A person who didn't want to be convinced — and was anyway — persuades more than an open pitch.

★ Recommended SCRIPT 03

Calling Mom

Framed as a video call home, pre-answering her mother's unspoken worries — safety, health, family, distance — one space at a time.

Takeaway: value measured in relationships, not amenities. Mirrors a conversation people actually have, which is why it travels.

SCRIPT 04

The List

She arrives with five self-imposed non-negotiables and tries to stay skeptical while the property clears every item, one by one.

Takeaway: value through rigor. Framing herself as a critical evaluator makes every "checked" land as more credible than enthusiasm alone — the strongest fit for a high-trust purchase decision like real estate.

Script Excerpt

"Calling Mom" — Full Scene

An excerpt from the recommended script, showing the video-call framing in action — including the pivot moment where the persuasion turns sincere.

"Calling Mom" — Excerpt Full script: 100–110 sec · Vertical · UGC format
0:00–0:12 — HOOK: "CALLING MOM"
Phone propped up, video-calling her mom mid-walk, slightly distracted by surroundings.
"Ma, hi — okay quick, I'm at this place I'm thinking of moving to, can I just... show you instead of explaining it? You're gonna ask fifty questions anyway."
1:06–1:24 — THE FUTURE-TENSE MOMENT
Tone softens significantly, walking slower near the play area, more thoughtful.
(quiet, smiling, slightly vulnerable) "Ma, look at this — there's a kids' play zone right in the community. I know, I know, way too early for that conversation."
"But I just thought — if I ever do have a family here one day, they'd just walk outside and be safe. That's a nice thing to imagine, no?"
1:40–1:50 — CLOSE
(warm, a little teary in a sweet way) "My Home Sayuk, Tellapur. I think I actually found home. Come see it with me before I sign anything, okay?"

Why this structure: the entire emotional weight comes from the creator anticipating and answering her mother's unspoken concerns — safety, health, family, distance — one location at a time. The kids' play area beat is the emotional peak, where the persuasive framing drops and she speaks plainly.
